Air Force

What is the Air Force?

The U.S. Air Force is the most deadly and powerful Air Force in the world, founded on September 18, 1947. The U.S. has 59 Active Reserve Bases ready to fly and ready to go on missions in less than 10 minutes. The Air Force carries with a huge variety of aircraft and carriers. Their aircraft range from training aircraft to bombers such as the B-2 Spirit. There are three main bombers in the Air Force that are known as the Trilogy. They consist of the B-1 Lancer, B-2 Spirit, and the B-52 Stratofortress.

Biggest Achievement

The biggest achievement made by the U.S. Air Force was the invention of the nuclear bomb. This achievement took place in the US in Los Alamos, New Mexico on the Alamogordo Bombing Range, also known as the Jornada Del Muerto. The creation was hurried as, during World War II, Japan attacked Pearl Harbor in Hawaii. After the nuclear bomb was created, it became the first nuke ever made and the US dropped 2 of them in Japan in the towns of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. After that, Japan decided to give up and retire from the war that the US went on to win with the end of the USSR and the end of Hitler's ruling in Germany.

How to Join The Air Force?

To enlist in the Air Force, there are 4 steps you must follow:

  1. Apply - Apply through a recruiter in your area and go over what position or job you want to apply for, once you pass their requirements, you will be moved onto testing.
  2. Aptitude Testing - you will have to take the Armed Services Vocational Aptitude Battery (ASVAB) as it helps you find whats your position depending on your skills and find out if you're ready for the demands of the Air Force.
  3. Physical and Mental Screening - After you have taken your ASVAB, you will have to schedule an appointment through your recruiter to a local Military Entrance Processing Station (MEPS) where you will be assessed based on your physical and mental moral standards set by the U.S. Air Force and the Department of Defense and Federal Law.
  4. Prepare for Basic Military Training - After having pass all the necessary requirements and accepted into the U.S. Air Force you will be placed into the Delayed Entry Programn (DEP) where you will wait for your departure date for Basic Military Training (BMT).
