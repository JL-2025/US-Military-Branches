What is the National Guard?

The National Guard is the reserve of the Army and Air Force for the United States of America and the U.S. Department of Defense. The National Guard also always have active personnel ready for when disaster strikes, not only in the U.S; but all over the world. How to Join The National Guard?

To enlist in the National Guard, there are 6 steps you must follow:

  1. Fill Out A Contact Form - You must first fill out a form the which recruiters will look over and analyse if you are eligible to move on and continue to the next step.
  2. Take An Eligibility Test - Once your recruiter deems you fit for the National Guard, you will have to take the Armed Services Vocational Aptitude Battery (ASVAB) test, your results will determine which jobs you qualify for.
  3. Enlist - After passing the Eligibility Test, you will have to confirm dates and information such as Basic Combat Training Ship Date, confirm your Guard job and pay, and take an oath of enlistment.
  4. Split Training Option - You will have to enlist while you're still a junior (you must be 17 years old to enlist and have permission from a parent or guardian), finish that year and attend Basic Combat Training during the summer between your junior and senior years.
  5. Prepare For Basic Training - Until the day that you get hsipped out, you will have to attend the Recruit Sustainment Program (RSP) for atleast one weekend, every month.
  6. Ship Out - You will have to complete Basic Combat Training for 10 weeks and you will officially part of the United States National Guard Reserve
